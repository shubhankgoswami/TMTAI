# Claude vs ChatGPT: The Differences That Actually Matter

If you've used ChatGPT for a year, you don't need to relearn how to use an AI. Most of what you know transfers. The 80% is the same: write a prompt, get an answer, iterate.

The 20% that's different is what trips senior users up. Three things matter:

**1. Claude is more literal and less hedging.** ChatGPT has been trained to wrap answers in caveats, suggestions, and conversational filler. Claude is more likely to give you the direct answer you asked for. If you ask for a one-line summary, you get a one-line summary. This is mostly good — but it means you need to be more precise about what you want, because Claude will take you at your word.

**2. Claude handles long documents materially better.** With a 200k token context window (~500 pages), Claude can read an entire deck, transcript, or proposal at once and answer cross-cutting questions. ChatGPT works with long content but tends to lose track of the middle. For BCG work — where you're often working with full client materials — this is the single biggest practical difference.

**3. Refusal behavior is different.** Claude refuses differently than ChatGPT does. It's less paranoid about hypothetical risks but more contextually careful about real ones (sensitive personal data, certain legal/medical scenarios). If you hit a refusal, the fix is usually clearer framing, not the same prompt with "please" added.

**The watch-out:** Don't paste your ChatGPT prompts wholesale. Most will work fine, but the ones that lean on ChatGPT's verbose-by-default style will produce thinner outputs in Claude. Strip the throat-clearing. Be specific about format and length.
