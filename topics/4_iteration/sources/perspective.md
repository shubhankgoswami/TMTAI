# Iteration: Your First Prompt Is a Scaffold, Not a Deliverable

Most BCG users treat Claude like a vending machine: drop in a prompt, expect a finished answer, walk away when it's only 70% right. The 70% is the trap — good enough to feel useful, bad enough to be unusable, polished enough that you don't notice what's missing until a partner reads it.

The shift: stop treating the first output as the deliverable. Treat it as a draft you're now reviewing with the model still in the room.

This single habit separates the BCG users getting real value from the ones getting frustrated. It's not about cleverer prompts. It's about staying in the loop instead of bouncing at turn one.

**Two highest-leverage moves:**

1. **Show your bar.** Paste an example of a slide or memo you consider partner-grade *before* asking for output. The model has no idea what BCG-quality means until you show it. This single move closes ~half the gap on first generation.

2. **Ask Claude to critique its own work.** After any output, say: "Critique this as a Senior Partner reviewing a PL's draft. What's weak? What's missing?" Then ask it to revise based on its own critique. Models evaluate better than they generate — use that.

**The line to remember:** Real output lives in turns 2 through 5. Plan 4-5 turns into your workflow, not one. The total time is roughly the same — but the first 80% happens in the first 10 minutes, and you spend the rest sharpening rather than typing from a blank page.
