Length target: 90 seconds. Hard cap 2 minutes. No filler.

Audience: BCG senior leaders — Project Leaders, Partners, Senior Partners. They use ChatGPT or Claude casually and treat it as a one-shot tool.

Tone: partner-to-partner. Terse. Confident. No hype. No "AI is amazing." The vibe is "here's the one habit that separates people getting real value from people getting frustrated."

Structure (no deviation):
- Hook (10s): The mistake — they treat Claude like a vending machine. First prompt in, finished answer expected. The 70%-right output is the trap.
- Insight (40s): The shift — stop reading the first output as the answer. Read it as a draft you're now reviewing with the model still in the room. Real output lives in turns 2 through 5.
- BCG example (30s): One concrete example — drafting a steerco slide. Show the move: paste an exemplary slide as "the bar" before asking for a draft. Then critique → revise. Go deep on this one example.
- Takeaway (10s): "Plan 4-5 turns, not one. Your first prompt is a scaffold, not a deliverable."

Hard rules:
- ONE idea. ONE example. No lists of techniques.
- No LLM jargon (tokens, embeddings, RAG, etc.)
- No vendor comparisons (ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.)
- No multi-point enumeration beyond the structure above
