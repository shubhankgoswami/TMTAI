# The Context Window: Stop Pasting Snippets

Most senior leaders use Claude like a chatbot: small prompts, small attachments, short conversations. That's the chatbot habit from ChatGPT-era usage. It leaves the most valuable capability on the table.

**Claude's context window — its working memory — is roughly 200,000 tokens, or about 500 pages.** That's the entire client deck. The entire interview transcript. The full RFP. The whole proposal plus all annexes. Together, in one conversation.

The mental shift: **Claude isn't a chatbot you ask quick questions. It's a workspace you load documents into.**

Once you make this shift, three things become possible that simply aren't possible in a chatbot mental model:

**1. Cross-cutting questions.** Drop the entire client RFP and your draft response. Ask: "Which requirements am I not addressing?" Claude reads both end-to-end and tells you. No chunking. No "summarize this section." Just the answer.

**2. Synthesis across documents.** Paste five interview transcripts. Ask: "What did the executives disagree about?" Claude can hold all five in working memory and identify tensions you'd miss reading them serially.

**3. Document-as-context conversations.** Once a document is loaded, you can have a 20-minute conversation about it. Each follow-up question stays grounded in the original material. You're not re-pasting, re-explaining, or losing thread.

**Practical rule:** If you're tempted to paste a "summary" or a "section" — paste the whole thing instead. The model is fine with it. You'll get better answers. The work is in asking the right questions of the full corpus, not in pre-digesting it for the model.

This is the single biggest workflow change from chatbot-era usage to document-era usage. Make this shift and Claude becomes a fundamentally different tool.
