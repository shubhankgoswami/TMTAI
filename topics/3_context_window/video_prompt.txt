Length target: 90 seconds. Hard cap 2 minutes. No filler.

Audience: BCG senior leaders who use Claude like a chatbot — short prompts, small attachments. They're leaving the biggest capability on the table. They want to be educated, not just told — give them enough so they can sound credible explaining this to a CTO or a colleague.

Tone: partner-to-partner. Terse. Confident. The vibe is "you're using this wrong; here's the one shift that changes everything, and here's the background you need to actually explain why."

Structure (no deviation):

- Hook (10s): The mistake — most people paste snippets and summaries. They treat Claude like a chatbot. Wrong mental model.

- Definition (20s): What a context window actually is — the model's working memory. The total volume of text it can hold during a single conversation: your prompt, the document you pasted, everything the model has said back. All of it shares one budget, measured in tokens (~3/4 of a word each). Once you blow past the limit, the earliest content falls out of memory.

- The "lost in the middle" backstory (20s): Why this used to be a problem — older models like GPT-3.5 famously suffered from a "lost in the middle" issue. They paid attention to the beginning and end of a long document but missed the middle. That's a documented research finding, not anecdote. Newer frontier models including Claude have been specifically trained to read the full window evenly. Window size matters, but attending evenly matters as much.

- Insight + BCG example (30s): Claude's window is ~200,000 tokens, about 500 pages, and it reads the middle. So Claude isn't a chatbot you ask quick questions — it's a workspace you load documents into. Concrete example: pasting the entire client RFP plus your draft response, then asking "which requirements am I not addressing yet?" That's a question you cannot ask a chatbot. It requires the model to hold both documents end-to-end in working memory simultaneously.

- Takeaway (10s): "If you're tempted to paste a summary or a section — paste the whole thing instead. The work is in the question, not the pre-digestion."

Hard rules:
- The definition and lost-in-the-middle backstory are mandatory — they're the educational layer that makes senior leaders credible on this topic.
- ONE concrete example (the RFP one).
- No vendor comparisons beyond the GPT-3.5 historical reference.
- Don't enumerate three use cases — pick the RFP example and go deep.
- The mental model shift (chatbot → workspace) is the spine of the video.
