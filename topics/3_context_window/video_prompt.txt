Length target: 90 seconds. Hard cap 2 minutes. No filler.

Audience: BCG senior leaders who use Claude like a chatbot — short prompts, small attachments. They're leaving the biggest capability on the table.

Tone: partner-to-partner. Terse. Confident. The vibe is "you're using this wrong and here's the one shift that changes everything."

Structure (no deviation):
- Hook (10s): The mistake — people paste snippets and summaries. They treat Claude like a chatbot. Wrong mental model.
- Insight (40s): The shift — Claude's context window is ~200,000 tokens, about 500 pages. The entire client deck. The entire RFP. The full transcript stack. Together. So Claude isn't a chatbot you ask quick questions. It's a workspace you load documents into.
- BCG example (30s): One concrete example — pasting the entire client RFP plus your draft response, then asking "which requirements am I not addressing yet?" Claude reads both end-to-end. No chunking, no pre-summarizing, just the answer. That's a question you can't ask a chatbot.
- Takeaway (10s): "If you're tempted to paste a summary or a section — paste the whole thing instead. The work is in the question, not the pre-digestion."

Hard rules:
- ONE example. The RFP one.
- No token math, no technical depth on what tokens are.
- No vendor comparisons.
- Don't list three use cases — pick the cleanest example and go deep.
- The mental model shift (chatbot → workspace) is the spine of the video.
